# Situation-Specific Executive Decision Rights Framework

## Crisis vs. Normal Operations Decision Authority

### **Technology Crisis Decision Rights**

#### **System Outage / Performance Crisis**
```
Situation: Customer-impacting system degradation, revenue at risk >$1M/day

CTO Immediate Authority (0-4 hours):
- Infrastructure spending: Up to $5M without approval for emergency capacity
- Team mobilization: 100% engineering team reallocation, cross-functional resources
- Vendor emergency engagement: Cloud providers, consulting firms, specialized contractors
- Architecture decisions: Temporary fixes, load balancing, traffic rerouting
- Customer communication: Technical status updates, timeline estimates

Escalation Triggers (4+ hours):
- CEO notification: If resolution timeline >24 hours or customer churn risk
- Board notification: If revenue impact >$5M or competitive advantage threatened
- Public disclosure: If material impact to public company or regulatory requirements

Example Scenario: E-commerce platform crashes during Black Friday
- CTO authority: $3M emergency cloud capacity, 60 engineers mobilized, external consultants
- No approval needed for: Premium vendor rates, overtime authorization, customer communication
- CEO escalation: After 6 hours if resolution uncertain, revenue impact >$10M
```

#### **Security Breach Response**
```
Situation: Confirmed data breach, potential regulatory notification required

CISO Immediate Authority (0-2 hours):
- Network isolation: Complete system shutdown authority if containment required
- External expertise: IR firms ($500K/week), forensic specialists, legal counsel
- Evidence preservation: System imaging, log collection, chain of custody procedures
- Regulatory pre-notification: Attorney-client privileged initial regulatory contact

CTO Coordination Authority (2-8 hours):
- Infrastructure resources: Emergency capacity for isolation and recovery systems
- Technical response: System rebuilding, security hardening, monitoring enhancement
- Data recovery: Backup system activation, clean environment preparation
- Integration with CISO: Technical implementation of security containment decisions

CEO/Board Notification Requirements:
- Immediate (0-2 hours): Confirmed breach with >1000 records or regulated data
- 4 hours: Preliminary impact assessment and response plan
- 24 hours: Comprehensive briefing with regulatory notification timeline

Example Scenario: Healthcare company breach with HIPAA data exposure
- CISO authority: $2M external IR firm, complete network isolation, DOJ notification prep
- CTO authority: $1M clean environment setup, 40 engineers on response team
- CEO escalation: Immediate due to regulatory requirements, patient notification required
```

### **Financial Crisis Decision Rights**

#### **Liquidity Crisis Management**
```
Situation: Cash runway <90 days, potential covenant breach within 60 days

CFO Immediate Authority (0-24 hours):
- Facility utilization: Draw down all available credit lines and facilities
- Cash acceleration: Customer payment acceleration, supplier payment delays
- Emergency financing: Asset-based lending up to $25M, factoring arrangements
- Vendor negotiations: Payment term extensions, service level modifications
- Banking relationships: Direct lender communication, waiver request initiation

CEO Coordination Authority (24-72 hours):
- Strategic alternatives: M&A discussions, partnership negotiations, investor outreach
- Operational changes: Workforce adjustments, facility closures, service reductions
- Customer communication: Service continuity assurance, relationship preservation
- Board management: Crisis communication, director liability discussion, strategic options

Board Approval Required:
- Equity dilution: >25% ownership dilution through emergency financing
- Asset sales: >$50M in strategic asset sales or core business divestiture
- Restructuring: Chapter 11 filing or out-of-court restructuring agreement
- Management changes: CEO replacement or C-suite restructuring

Example Scenario: SaaS company with declining ARR, 75-day cash runway
- CFO authority: $15M credit line draw, 45-day supplier payment delay, $8M factoring
- CEO authority: 30% workforce reduction, customer retention program, investor outreach
- Board approval: $40M emergency equity round at 50% discount to last financing
```

#### **Covenant Breach Response**
```
Situation: Leverage covenant breach confirmed, 30-day cure period

CFO Immediate Authority (0-7 days):
- Lender communication: Direct relationship manager contact, waiver request preparation
- Legal counsel: Restructuring attorneys, workout specialists, covenant analysis
- Financial modeling: Revised projections, compliance scenarios, alternative structures
- Covenant compliance actions: Asset sales, debt paydown, EBITDA improvement initiatives

Extended Authority (7-30 days):
- Waiver negotiations: Fee payments up to $5M, pricing adjustments, covenant modifications
- Alternative financing: Refinancing preparation, new lender discussions, bridge financing
- Operational coordination: Work with CEO/COO on performance improvement initiatives
- Stakeholder communication: Rating agency discussions, investor relations, audit committee

CEO/Board Involvement:
- Strategic decisions: Major operational changes required for covenant compliance
- Negotiation support: CEO relationship leverage with senior bank management
- Board approval: Significant waiver fees, permanent covenant modifications, refinancing

Example Scenario: Manufacturing company, 4.5x leverage vs 4.0x covenant
- CFO authority: $2M waiver fee payment, asset sale initiation for $20M proceeds
- CEO involvement: Operational improvement plan, customer contract acceleration
- Board approval: Required for >$3M in waiver fees and permanent covenant changes
```

### **Operational Crisis Decision Rights**

#### **Supply Chain Disruption Response**
```
Situation: Critical supplier bankruptcy, affects 40% of production capacity

COO Immediate Authority (0-48 hours):
- Alternative sourcing: Emergency supplier qualification, premium pricing acceptance
- Production adjustment: Line reallocation, product mix changes, capacity utilization
- Inventory management: Safety stock deployment, finished goods prioritization
- Customer communication: Delivery timeline updates, priority customer protection
- Logistics: Expedited shipping, alternative transportation, warehouse reallocation

CFO Coordination Authority (48 hours - 2 weeks):
- Financial exposure: Supplier receivables recovery, alternative financing for inventory
- Working capital: Inventory investment for supply security, payment term negotiations
- Cost management: Premium sourcing cost absorption, margin impact assessment
- Customer contracts: Force majeure evaluation, penalty negotiations, pricing adjustments

CEO Strategic Authority (1-4 weeks):
- Supplier relationships: Strategic partnership negotiations, acquisition discussions
- Market positioning: Customer expectation management, competitive response planning
- Long-term strategy: Supply chain diversification, vertical integration evaluation
- Stakeholder communication: Board briefing, investor relations, analyst discussions

Example Scenario: Automotive parts supplier bankruptcy affects 60% of component supply
- COO authority: $8M premium supplier contracts, production line reallocation
- CFO authority: $15M inventory buildup, customer contract force majeure claims
- CEO authority: Strategic supplier acquisition discussions, board briefing on supply strategy
```

#### **Quality Crisis Management**
```
Situation: Product recall required, customer safety risk, regulatory investigation

COO Immediate Authority (0-12 hours):
- Production halt: Complete product line shutdown, inventory quarantine
- Quality investigation: Root cause analysis team, external quality consultants
- Regulatory compliance: Initial regulatory notification, recall preparation
- Customer safety: Immediate customer notification, usage suspension advisory
- Supply chain: Vendor investigation, component testing, supplier audit acceleration

CEO Crisis Leadership (12-48 hours):
- Public communication: Media strategy, customer communication, stakeholder briefing
- Regulatory strategy: Regulatory relationship management, legal defense coordination
- Financial impact: Recall cost estimation, insurance claims, revenue impact assessment
- Reputation management: PR firm engagement, crisis communication, customer retention

Board Involvement:
- Material disclosure: Public company SEC reporting, material event notification
- Strategic decisions: Product line discontinuation, market exit, acquisition impact
- Leadership accountability: Management responsibility assessment, investor relations
- Legal strategy: Litigation defense, settlement authority, D&O insurance claims

Example Scenario: Food manufacturer contamination, FDA investigation, customer illness reports
- COO authority: $5M immediate recall costs, production shutdown, FDA cooperation
- CEO authority: Public apology, customer remediation program, crisis PR firm
- Board approval: >$25M recall and legal reserve, potential management changes
```

## Investment Decision Authority by Size and Strategic Impact

### **Technology Investment Decision Rights**

#### **Strategic Platform Investment ($10M+ / Strategic Significance)**
```
Decision Framework:
- Market-defining technology (AI, quantum, blockchain first-mover)
- Competitive moat creation or defense
- Business model transformation enablement
- Regulatory compliance requiring new capabilities

CTO Analysis Authority:
- Technical feasibility assessment: Architecture evaluation, proof of concept development
- Competitive analysis: Technology landscape, patent freedom to operate, vendor evaluation
- Resource requirements: Team size, budget, timeline, success probability assessment
- Implementation planning: Phased approach, risk mitigation, milestone definitions

CEO Strategic Review:
- Business case validation: Revenue impact, competitive positioning, strategic alignment
- Resource prioritization: Investment vs. other strategic initiatives, opportunity cost
- Market timing: Competitive window, technology maturity, customer readiness
- Stakeholder communication: Board presentation, investor relations, analyst briefing

Board Approval Required:
- Investment >$25M or >15% of annual R&D budget
- Technology acquisition >$100M or strategic partnership with equity
- Platform pivot affecting core business model or competitive positioning
- International expansion requiring new technology infrastructure

Example Decision: AI-First Platform Transformation
- CTO assessment: $35M investment, 24-month timeline, 70% technical success probability
- CEO evaluation: Competitive necessity, 18-month market window, customer demand validated
- Board decision: Approved with $40M budget including contingency, quarterly review milestones
```

#### **Operational Technology Investment ($1-10M / Efficiency Focus)**
```
Decision Framework:
- Productivity improvement, cost reduction, operational efficiency
- Infrastructure scaling, system reliability, performance optimization
- Regulatory compliance, security enhancement, technical debt reduction

CTO Full Authority (with CEO notification):
- Infrastructure scaling: Cloud capacity, database upgrades, network enhancement
- Security investments: Tools, consulting, compliance requirements
- Technical debt: Refactoring, modernization, platform maintenance
- Team development: Training, tools, productivity enhancement

CFO Coordination Required:
- ROI validation: Cost savings quantification, payback period, NPV calculation
- Budget impact: Capital vs. operational expense, cash flow timing, financing approach
- Vendor negotiations: Contract terms, payment schedules, service level agreements
- Performance measurement: Success metrics, tracking systems, accountability framework

CEO Involvement Triggers:
- Investment >$5M or integration affecting multiple business units
- Customer-facing system changes with revenue or satisfaction impact
- Vendor relationships affecting strategic partnerships or competitive positioning
- Timeline >18 months or requiring significant organizational change

Example Decision: Data Center Modernization
- CTO authority: $3M infrastructure investment, 12-month implementation, 40% efficiency gain
- CFO validation: 18-month payback, 25% IRR, positive cash flow impact by month 15
- CEO notification: Strategic vendor partnership, customer performance improvement
```

### **Financial Investment Decision Rights**

#### **M&A Transaction Authority**
```
Transaction Size-Based Authority:

$0-25M Transactions:
- CFO lead authority: Financial due diligence, valuation, deal structuring
- CEO approval: Strategic fit, integration capability, board notification
- Board information: Transaction summary, strategic rationale, integration plan

$25-100M Transactions:
- CEO lead authority: Strategic vision, negotiation leadership, stakeholder management
- CFO execution: Financial analysis, due diligence, deal structure optimization
- Board approval: Required for transaction execution, financing, integration investment

$100M+ Transactions:
- Board lead authority: Strategic committee oversight, special committee if conflicts
- CEO execution: Day-to-day negotiation, management presentation, integration planning
- Shareholder approval: If >25% of company value or stock consideration

Strategic Acquisition (regardless of size):
- Competitive positioning: Blocking competitor access, market consolidation
- Technology acquisition: Platform enhancement, talent acquisition, IP portfolio
- Market expansion: Geographic extension, customer base access, channel development

Example Scenarios:
$15M Acqui-hire: CTO identifies, CFO structures, CEO approves, board notification
$60M Strategic: CEO leads, board approves, special dividend committee if needed
$200M Merger: Board special committee, shareholder vote, regulatory approval coordination
```

#### **Capital Structure Decisions**
```
Financing Decision Authority:

Debt Financing:
- <$25M: CFO authority with CEO notification, standard commercial terms
- $25-100M: CEO approval required, board notification, covenant review
- >$100M: Board approval, potential rating agency interaction, material disclosure

Equity Financing:
- <10% dilution: CEO authority with board notification (private companies)
- 10-25% dilution: Board approval required, anti-dilution provisions, valuation validation
- >25% dilution: Special committee, fairness opinion, potential control implications

Strategic Alternatives:
- Dividend policy: Board approval for changes affecting >20% of cash flow
- Share repurchase: Board authorization, SEC compliance, insider trading considerations
- Spin-off/divestiture: Board special committee, shareholder approval, tax implications

Crisis Financing:
- Emergency authority: CFO up to $50M, CEO up to $100M, board unlimited
- Distressed situations: Restructuring counsel, creditor negotiations, stakeholder management
- Going concern: Audit considerations, disclosure requirements, liquidity planning

Example Authority: $75M Credit Facility Refinancing
- CFO analysis: Market conditions, pricing comparison, covenant negotiation
- CEO approval: Relationship management, strategic terms, board presentation
- Board notification: Material change in capital structure, covenant modifications
```

## Stakeholder-Specific Decision Authority

### **Customer-Impacting Decisions**

#### **Service Level/Pricing Changes**
```
Decision Authority by Customer Impact:

Individual Customer (<$1M ARR):
- Sales manager: Standard contract terms, discount approval within guidelines
- VP Sales: Non-standard terms, pricing exceptions, service level modifications
- CEO involvement: Strategic accounts, competitive situations, retention risk

Major Customer ($1-10M ARR):
- VP Sales: Contract negotiations, pricing strategy, relationship management
- CEO approval: Material terms changes, multi-year commitments, strategic partnerships
- Board notification: >$5M annual impact, strategic relationship changes

Enterprise Customer (>$10M ARR):
- CEO leadership: Negotiation strategy, relationship management, strategic alignment
- Board involvement: >$25M contracts, strategic partnerships, competitive implications
- Legal approval: Contract terms, liability, intellectual property, regulatory compliance

Service Level Changes:
- Minor changes (<5% impact): Operations manager authority
- Material changes (5-20% impact): COO approval with CEO notification
- Major changes (>20% impact): CEO approval with board notification

Example Scenario: SLA Modification for Top Customer
- Customer impact: $15M ARR, requesting 99.99% vs. standard 99.9% uptime
- Authority level: CEO negotiation, CTO feasibility assessment, CFO cost analysis
- Board involvement: If infrastructure investment >$5M or competitive precedent risk
```

#### **Product/Service Discontinuation**
```
Discontinuation Decision Authority:

Minor Feature/Service:
- Product manager: Usage analysis, customer communication, alternative recommendation
- VP Product: Strategic impact, competitive implications, resource reallocation
- CEO notification: If customer complaints or competitive differentiation impact

Major Product Line:
- CEO leadership: Strategic decision, customer migration planning, financial impact
- Board approval: If >15% of revenue or strategic market positioning
- Customer communication: Executive-level notification, migration support, retention programs

Business Unit Divestiture:
- Board authority: Strategic committee oversight, valuation analysis, stakeholder impact
- CEO execution: Buyer identification, negotiation management, employee communication
- Regulatory approval: Antitrust considerations, customer notification, compliance requirements

Example Decision: Legacy Product End-of-Life
- Product impact: $8M annual revenue, 200 customers, declining 30% annually
- Authority level: CEO decision with board notification, 18-month customer migration
- Communication: Executive customer calls, migration incentives, alternative solutions
```

### **Employee-Impacting Decisions**

#### **Organizational Changes**
```
Change Authority by Employee Impact:

Individual Changes:
- Manager authority: Performance management, role changes, development planning
- HR approval: Policy exceptions, accommodation requests, investigation outcomes
- Executive approval: Senior role changes, retention packages, special arrangements

Team/Department Changes (<50 people):
- Department head: Team restructuring, role redefinition, skills development
- Functional C-Suite: Budget impact, strategic alignment, change management
- CEO notification: If customer impact or strategic capability implications

Division Changes (50-500 people):
- Functional C-Suite: Organizational design, change management, communication strategy
- CEO approval: Strategic implications, financial impact, stakeholder management
- Board notification: If >10% workforce or material business impact

Enterprise Changes (>500 people):
- CEO leadership: Strategic vision, stakeholder communication, implementation oversight
- Board approval: Material impact, competitive positioning, financial implications
- Regulatory compliance: WARN act, international labor laws, union negotiations

Example Scenario: Sales Organization Restructuring
- Impact: 150 sales professionals, territory realignment, compensation changes
- Authority: VP Sales design, CEO approval, board notification of revenue impact
- Implementation: 90-day timeline, retention packages, customer relationship management
```

#### **Compensation and Benefits Changes**
```
Compensation Decision Authority:

Individual Compensation:
- Manager: Merit increases within budget guidelines (typically 0-10%)
- HR approval: Off-cycle increases, promotion adjustments, retention packages
- Executive approval: Above-guideline increases, equity grants, special arrangements

Program Changes:
- HR leadership: Policy development, market analysis, program design
- Executive committee: Budget impact, competitive positioning, implementation timeline
- Board approval: Executive compensation, equity plans, material benefit changes

Crisis Compensation:
- Emergency authority: CEO up to $10M for retention during crisis or acquisition
- Retention programs: Functional executives for key talent within departments
- Severance programs: CEO approval for enhanced packages, legal compliance coordination

Example Decision: Engineering Retention Program
- Challenge: 25% annual turnover, competitive market, key project risk
- Authority: CTO program design, CEO budget approval ($5M), board notification
- Implementation: Market-rate adjustments, equity refresh, career development investment
```

Each decision rights framework includes:
- **Specific dollar thresholds** and impact criteria for different authority levels
- **Escalation triggers** based on risk, complexity, and stakeholder impact
- **Time-based authority** with crisis override protocols
- **Cross-functional coordination** requirements for complex decisions
- **Documentation and communication** requirements for accountability and transparency
- **Success metrics** and adjustment mechanisms for continuous framework improvement
