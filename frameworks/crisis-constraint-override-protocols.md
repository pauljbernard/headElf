# Crisis Constraint-Override Protocols

## Executive Authority Escalation Framework

### **Crisis Classification and Authority Elevation**

#### **Crisis Severity Levels**
```
Level 1: Operational Crisis
- Impact: Single function/region affected, <$5M revenue risk, <48 hour resolution timeline
- Authority elevation: Department head → Functional C-Suite
- Constraint overrides: Budget +50%, resource reallocation within function, external vendor engagement
- Approval bypass: Skip standard procurement, accelerate hiring, emergency contracts

Level 2: Company Crisis
- Impact: Multiple functions affected, $5-50M revenue risk, 48 hours - 2 weeks resolution
- Authority elevation: C-Suite → CEO + Board Chair
- Constraint overrides: Budget +200%, cross-functional resource mobilization, external expert engagement
- Approval bypass: Board meeting protocols, standard approval chains, regulatory notification delays

Level 3: Existential Crisis
- Impact: Company survival threatened, >$50M exposure, immediate action required
- Authority elevation: CEO → Board + External advisors
- Constraint overrides: Unlimited budget authority, any resource mobilization, emergency financing
- Approval bypass: All standard processes, fiduciary duty focus, stakeholder notification acceleration
```

#### **Time-Based Authority Progression**
```
Hour 0-2: Individual Executive Authority
- CTO: $2M infrastructure spending, 100% team reallocation, vendor emergency engagement
- CFO: $10M liquidity access, banking relationships activation, legal counsel engagement
- CISO: Full network isolation authority, external IR firm engagement, law enforcement notification
- COO: Production halt authority, supply chain emergency protocols, customer communication

Hour 2-8: Cross-Functional Authority
- Any C-Suite: Can direct other functional resources, override departmental priorities
- Resource mobilization: All available internal resources, external contractor surge
- Communication authority: Customer/supplier notification, regulatory pre-briefing
- Financial authority: Emergency expenditure up to $25M, facility activation

Hour 8-24: CEO Emergency Authority
- Budget: Unlimited operational spending, emergency financing activation
- Personnel: Any staff reallocation, executive team restructuring, external expert engagement
- Stakeholder: Board notification, customer communication, regulatory engagement
- Strategic: Business model changes, market repositioning, partnership activation

Day 1+: Board Emergency Powers
- Corporate structure: Subsidiary isolation, asset protection, liability management
- Financial: Emergency financing, asset sales, restructuring initiation
- Strategic: Business model pivot, market exit, acquisition/merger consideration
- Governance: Management changes, board composition, external oversight acceptance
```

### **Budget and Resource Override Protocols**

#### **Financial Constraint Suspension**
```
Crisis Spending Authority:

Immediate Access (0-4 hours):
- CTO: $5M infrastructure, cloud capacity, external consulting
- CFO: $20M liquidity management, facility activation, professional services
- CISO: $10M security response, forensics, external IR teams
- COO: $15M operational continuity, supply chain, customer impact mitigation

Extended Authority (4-48 hours):
- Any C-Suite: $50M for crisis resolution activities
- Combined authority: $100M for coordinated response
- External financing: Emergency credit facilities, asset-based lending activation
- Professional services: Unlimited crisis consulting, legal, advisory services

Reporting Requirements:
- Real-time: CEO notification of >$1M expenditures
- 4-hour: Board summary of total crisis spending and projections
- 24-hour: Detailed expenditure report with justification and ongoing requirements
- Weekly: Full financial impact assessment and recovery cost projections
```

#### **Resource Reallocation Override**
```
Personnel Mobilization:

Immediate Reallocation (0-2 hours):
- 100% of functional team can be redirected to crisis response
- Cross-functional teams can be formed without management approval
- External contractors can be engaged at premium rates (2-3x standard)
- Consultants and advisors can be activated without procurement process

Extended Mobilization (2-24 hours):
- Any employee can be temporarily reassigned to crisis response
- Offshore teams can be activated for 24/7 response coverage
- Executive assistants become crisis coordinators and communication hubs
- Board members can be activated for stakeholder communication

Resource Prioritization:
- Crisis response: 70% of available resources
- Business continuity: 25% of available resources
- Future planning: 5% of available resources (skeleton crew only)
- All discretionary projects suspended immediately

Vendor/Supplier Override:
- Payment terms acceleration: Net-30 becomes Net-15 for critical suppliers
- Contract modifications: Force majeure activation, SLA suspension
- Emergency procurement: Bypass competitive bidding, accept premium pricing
- Supplier relationship leveraging: Request extraordinary support, reciprocal commitments
```

### **Regulatory and Compliance Override Protocols**

#### **Legal and Regulatory Constraint Management**
```
Compliance Priority Reorganization:

Tier 1: Cannot be overridden (Legal/Safety)
- Employee safety requirements and OSHA compliance
- Financial reporting accuracy and anti-fraud measures
- Data privacy for personal information (GDPR, CCPA core requirements)
- Securities law disclosure obligations for material information

Tier 2: Temporary suspension authorized (Operational)
- Non-critical SOX controls (with documentation and remediation plan)
- Vendor qualification processes (with enhanced monitoring)
- Standard approval workflows (with post-crisis validation)
- Non-essential audit and compliance reporting timelines

Tier 3: Full suspension during crisis (Process)
- Discretionary policy compliance (travel, expense, procurement)
- Standard training and certification requirements
- Non-critical documentation and record-keeping
- Routine compliance reporting and committee meetings

Regulatory Communication Protocol:
- Immediate: Material event notification to SEC, relevant regulators
- 24 hours: Preliminary impact assessment and remediation timeline
- 72 hours: Detailed crisis response plan and compliance resumption schedule
- Weekly: Progress updates and compliance restoration milestones
```

#### **Stakeholder Notification Acceleration**
```
Communication Override Priorities:

Immediate Notification (0-2 hours):
- Board chair and key board members
- Primary banking relationships and credit facilities
- Key customers representing >10% revenue each
- Critical suppliers for operational continuity

Priority Notification (2-8 hours):
- Full board of directors
- All banking syndicate members
- Major customers representing >1% revenue each
- Regulatory bodies with jurisdiction over crisis type

Standard Notification (8-24 hours):
- All shareholders (if public company)
- Full customer and supplier base
- Employees and key stakeholders
- Media and public communications (if required)

Communication Authority Override:
- Legal review: Expedited to 1-hour maximum vs. standard 24-48 hours
- CEO approval: Not required for operational crisis communications
- Board approval: Not required for tactical crisis response communications
- PR/Marketing: Crisis communication takes priority over all other messaging
```

### **Decision-Making Process Acceleration**

#### **Approval Chain Compression**
```
Standard vs. Crisis Decision Authority:

Technology Decisions:
- Standard: Architecture committee → CTO → CEO (2-4 weeks)
- Crisis: CTO immediate authority → CEO notification within 4 hours
- Override examples: Emergency cloud capacity, system architecture changes, vendor switches

Financial Decisions:
- Standard: Budget approval → CFO → CEO → Board (4-8 weeks for >$10M)
- Crisis: CFO immediate authority up to $50M → Board notification within 24 hours
- Override examples: Emergency financing, supplier payments, crisis consulting

Operational Decisions:
- Standard: Department → COO → CEO (1-2 weeks)
- Crisis: COO immediate authority → CEO notification within 2 hours
- Override examples: Production changes, supply chain pivots, customer service modifications

Strategic Decisions:
- Standard: Strategic planning → CEO → Board (4-12 weeks)
- Crisis: CEO immediate authority → Board ratification within 48 hours
- Override examples: Market repositioning, partnership agreements, acquisition discussions
```

#### **Quality and Risk Acceptance Protocols**
```
Risk Tolerance Elevation:

Technical Risk Acceptance:
- Standard: 99.9% confidence in solution before implementation
- Crisis: 80% confidence acceptable with monitoring and rollback procedures
- Quality gates: Reduced testing, accelerated deployment, enhanced monitoring
- Failure tolerance: Accept 20% solution failure rate vs. standard <1%

Financial Risk Acceptance:
- Standard: Detailed ROI analysis, multiple scenario modeling
- Crisis: Directional analysis sufficient, bias toward action over analysis
- Investment decisions: Accept lower ROI thresholds for crisis resolution
- Liquidity management: Accept higher costs for certainty and speed

Operational Risk Acceptance:
- Standard: Comprehensive impact analysis, stakeholder alignment
- Crisis: Rapid impact assessment, bias toward operational continuity
- Customer impact: Accept temporary service degradation vs. system failure
- Supplier risk: Accept single-source dependencies for crisis resolution

Compliance Risk Acceptance:
- Standard: Full compliance with all applicable regulations
- Crisis: Focus on material compliance, document exceptions for later remediation
- Documentation: Maintain crisis decision log for post-crisis compliance review
- Regulatory engagement: Proactive communication about compliance modifications
```

### **Crisis Communication and Coordination**

#### **Information Flow Override**
```
Communication Protocol Acceleration:

Internal Communications:
- Executive updates: Every 2 hours during active crisis (vs. weekly standard)
- Board briefings: Daily during crisis (vs. quarterly standard)
- Employee updates: Every 4 hours during crisis (vs. monthly standard)
- Department coordination: Hourly status calls (vs. weekly meetings)

External Communications:
- Customer communications: 2-hour maximum delay for material impact
- Supplier notifications: Immediate for operational impact, 4 hours for planning
- Regulatory communications: As legally required, erring on side of early disclosure
- Media communications: Coordinated through CEO office, legal review expedited

Information Sharing Override:
- Standard confidentiality: Suspended for crisis response team members
- Need-to-know restrictions: Expanded to anyone involved in crisis resolution
- Competitive information: Can be shared with vendors/advisors under emergency NDAs
- Financial information: Can be shared with operational teams for decision-making context
```

#### **Decision Documentation Requirements**
```
Crisis Decision Record-Keeping:

Immediate Documentation (Real-time):
- Decision maker, timestamp, situation context
- Options considered, decision rationale, resource commitments
- Expected outcomes, success metrics, review timeline
- Approvals obtained or bypassed with justification

Daily Consolidation:
- Summary of all crisis decisions and expenditures
- Resource utilization and reallocation summary
- Stakeholder communications log and feedback
- Risk assessment updates and mitigation status

Weekly Assessment:
- Crisis response effectiveness evaluation
- Financial impact assessment and projections
- Stakeholder satisfaction and relationship status
- Lessons learned and process improvement recommendations

Post-Crisis Analysis:
- Complete crisis timeline and decision tree
- Financial impact reconciliation and recovery planning
- Compliance exception resolution and remediation
- Organization capability improvements and preparedness enhancement
```

## Role-Specific Crisis Authority

### **CTO Crisis Authority Expansion**
```
Technology Crisis Leadership:

Immediate Authority (0-4 hours):
- Infrastructure spending: $5M without approval
- Cloud capacity: Unlimited scaling, cost optimization later
- Vendor emergency engagement: Security firms, architecture consultants, system integrators
- Team reallocation: 100% engineering team, cross-functional technical resources

Extended Authority (4-24 hours):
- Architecture decisions: Complete system redesign if necessary
- Acquisition discussions: Technology acquisition for crisis resolution
- Partner agreements: Emergency technology partnerships, IP sharing if necessary
- Regulatory engagement: Direct communication with technical regulators

Ongoing Authority (1+ days):
- Strategic technology direction: Crisis-driven platform changes
- Investment priorities: Complete R&D reallocation for crisis resolution
- Organizational design: Engineering team restructuring, external team integration
- Market positioning: Technology-based competitive responses
```

### **CFO Crisis Authority Expansion**
```
Financial Crisis Leadership:

Immediate Authority (0-4 hours):
- Liquidity access: $25M from all available sources
- Banking relationships: Direct lender communication, facility modifications
- Payment prioritization: Supplier payment delays, customer payment acceleration
- Legal engagement: Restructuring counsel, crisis financial advisors

Extended Authority (4-24 hours):
- Financing decisions: Emergency debt, equity, asset sales up to $100M
- Investment bank engagement: Crisis financing, strategic alternative development
- Regulatory filings: Material event disclosure, compliance modifications
- Insurance claims: Directors and officers, business interruption, cyber liability

Ongoing Authority (1+ days):
- Capital structure: Comprehensive recapitalization, restructuring initiation
- Strategic alternatives: M&A discussions, partnership negotiations, investor relations
- Operational finance: Working capital optimization, cash management enhancement
- Stakeholder relations: Creditor negotiations, investor communications, rating agency engagement
```

### **CISO Crisis Authority Expansion**
```
Security Crisis Leadership:

Immediate Authority (0-2 hours):
- Network isolation: Complete system shutdown if necessary
- Forensic preservation: Evidence collection, chain of custody establishment
- External expertise: IR firms, law enforcement, regulatory notification
- Communication control: Information sharing restrictions, stakeholder notifications

Extended Authority (2-24 hours):
- Legal strategy: Privilege establishment, litigation hold, regulatory coordination
- Technical response: System rebuilding, architecture hardening, monitoring enhancement
- Vendor coordination: Security solution deployment, emergency consulting
- Organizational communication: Employee notification, customer communication, media strategy

Ongoing Authority (1+ days):
- Security transformation: Complete security architecture redesign
- Compliance management: Regulatory response, audit coordination, remediation planning
- Strategic planning: Business continuity, competitive positioning, market confidence restoration
- Industry coordination: Information sharing, industry collaboration, threat intelligence
```

Each override protocol includes:
- **Specific authority levels** with dollar amounts and resource commitments
- **Time-based escalation** with automatic authority expansion
- **Documentation requirements** for post-crisis review and compliance
- **Stakeholder communication** protocols with accelerated timelines
- **Risk acceptance frameworks** with quality and compliance trade-offs
- **Recovery planning** integration with business continuity requirements
